Carbon Reporting Policy Framework

A comprehensive guide to all national and international regulations governing industrial carbon emission disclosure in India — and how EcoMark ensures your facility is fully compliant across every framework.

India — Domestic Regulatory Mandates

Environment Protection Act, 1986 (EPA)

MoEF&CC — Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change

ACTIVE

Who It Applies To

All industrial facilities classified as "Red" or "Orange" category by CPCB/SPCB. Includes manufacturing, chemicals, food processing, textiles, steel, cement, paper.

What's Required

Annual environmental statement to SPCB. Consent to Operate (CTO) renewal requires emission compliance data. Facilities ≥250 employees must file Form V every year by 30th September.

EcoMark Support

EcoMark's quarterly emission reports are pre-formatted for Form V submission. Audit logs serve as supporting documentation for SPCB inspections.

PAT Scheme — Perform Achieve Trade (BEE)

Bureau of Energy Efficiency — Ministry of Power

CYCLE 7 ACTIVE

Designated Consumer (DC) Sectors

Aluminum, Cement, Chlor-Alkali, Fertilizer, Iron & Steel, Paper & Pulp, Petrochemicals, Petroleum Refinery, Textiles, Thermal Power Plants + 3 more.

What's Required

Each DC must achieve assigned Specific Energy Consumption (SEC) targets. Overachievers receive tradeable Energy Saving Certificates (ESCerts). Non-achievers must purchase ESCerts or pay penalties.

EcoMark Support

EcoMark's energy consumption analytics directly map to SEC calculation. Trend data helps DCs track progress against PAT targets quarterly, not just annually.

SEBI BRSR — Business Responsibility & Sustainability Report

Securities and Exchange Board of India — Circular SEBI/HO/CFD/CMD-2/P/CIR/2021/562

MANDATORY FY23

Who It Applies To

Top 1,000 listed companies by market cap (NSE/BSE). Mandatory since FY 2022-23. Extended to top-5,000 proposed by 2026 under SEBI's BRSR Core expansion.

Relevant BRSR Principles

Principle 6 (Environment): Energy consumption, GHG emissions (Scope 1 & 2), water intensity, waste generation. Leadership indicators require third-party verification for A-rated disclosures.

EcoMark Support

EcoMark's Scope 1 & 2 reports map to BRSR P6 Essential Indicators 1–7 verbatim. Export BRSR-formatted CSV for direct filing. Audit trail satisfies third-party verification requirement.

India NDC — Nationally Determined Contribution (Paris Agreement)

MoEF&CC — Updated NDC submitted to UNFCCC, August 2022

2030 TARGET

National Commitment

45% reduction in emission intensity of GDP by 2030 vs 2005 baseline. 50% cumulative electric power capacity from non-fossil sources by 2030. Net-zero by 2070.

Industrial Implication

The government will cascade NDC targets to state PCBs and sectoral regulators. Facilities without verified emission reduction data risk exclusion from government contracts and credit schemes post-2025.

EcoMark Support

Platform aggregates anonymized data for national MRV (Measurement, Reporting, Verification) dashboards, contributing directly to India's UNFCCC country reporting obligations.

International Standards & Frameworks

ISO
14064

ISO 14064-1:2018

Greenhouse Gas Quantification & Reporting at Organizational Level

The international standard for quantifying and reporting Scope 1 and Scope 2 GHG emissions. Specifies principles and requirements for designing, developing, managing, and reporting GHG inventories. Required by most global procurement sustainability audits.

EcoMark Alignment

IPCC emission factors used by EcoMark are the same coefficients referenced in ISO 14064-1 Annex B. Our report structure follows Clause 5.4 (Quantification) and Clause 7 (Documentation) verbatim.

GHG
PROTO

GHG Protocol — Corporate Accounting Standard

World Resources Institute (WRI) & World Business Council for Sustainable Development

World's most widely used GHG accounting standard. Defines Scope 1 (direct emissions), Scope 2 (indirect from purchased energy), and Scope 3 (value chain). Used as foundation for SEBI BRSR, CDP, TCFD, and SBTi reporting.

EcoMark Alignment

EcoMark covers GHG Protocol Scope 1 (fuel combustion) and Scope 2 (electricity) in full. Scope 3 module is on the product roadmap for Phase 2.

EU
CSRD

EU CSRD & ESRS E1 — Climate Change

Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive — Effective 2025

Requires EU-listed companies and non-EU companies with >€150M EU revenue to disclose climate-related risks and emissions under European Sustainability Reporting Standard E1. Applies to Indian MNCs with EU operations from 2026.

EcoMark Alignment

EcoMark's digital audit trail satisfies ESRS E1's "double materiality" verification requirement. Data exports can be uploaded directly to EFRAG's reporting template.

EU
CBAM

EU CBAM — Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism

Regulation (EU) 2023/956 — Full enforcement from 2026

Requires importers of steel, iron, cement, aluminum, electricity, fertilizers, and hydrogen into the EU to declare embedded carbon and purchase CBAM certificates. Affects ₹58,000+ Cr of Indian exports annually. Violators face import bans.

EcoMark Alignment

EcoMark-verified emission data for steel, aluminum, and cement facilities is pre-formatted for CBAM Annex IV declaration forms. Export directly from the Admin portal.

SBTi

SBTi — Science Based Targets Initiative

CDP, UN Global Compact, WRI, WWF joint initiative

Over 7,000 companies globally have committed to SBTi targets, including 200+ Indian companies. SBTi requires a 1.5°C-aligned emission reduction trajectory, independently validated. Required by major global supply chains (Apple, Microsoft, Unilever, Walmart).

EcoMark Alignment

EcoMark's trend analytics automatically calculate and visualize 5-year reduction trajectories, showing whether a company is on a 1.5°C, 2°C, or above path for SBTi submission.

TCFD

TCFD — Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures

Financial Stability Board — Incorporated into ISSB IFRS S2

TCFD recommends disclosure of governance, strategy, risk management, and metrics related to climate change. Now incorporated into IFRS Sustainability Disclosure Standards (ISSB S2), which RBI is aligning Indian banking sector to from 2025.

EcoMark Alignment

EcoMark's Metrics & Targets module provides the quantitative data required for TCFD Pillar 4. The audit trail satisfies the "robust verification" requirement for institutional investor reporting.

Penalties for Non-Compliance

warning Non-compliance is not a calculated risk — it is an operational liability.

Regulators globally are moving from voluntary to mandatory enforcement. The following penalties are currently enforceable for qualifying facilities.

Regulation Violation Penalty Severity
EPA 1986 (India) Failure to file annual environmental statement (Form V) ₹1 Lakh/day fine + potential imprisonment (1–5 years) + CTO cancellation CRITICAL
BEE PAT Scheme Failure to meet Specific Energy Consumption (SEC) target Must purchase ESCerts or pay ₹10 per kWh equivalent penalty. Repeat: facility shutdown orders HIGH
SEBI BRSR Non-filing or materially incorrect BRSR for listed companies SEBI penalty up to ₹25 Cr + delisting risk + credit rating downgrade triggers CRITICAL
EU CBAM Exporting regulated goods to EU without CBAM certificate €50 per tonne CO2 undeclared + import confiscation + blacklisting from EU markets CRITICAL
EU CSRD Non-disclosure or false sustainability reporting (EU operations) Up to 5% of global annual turnover. Class-action shareholder suits permitted HIGH
India NDC (post-2025) Facilities unable to demonstrate emission intensity reduction Progressive carbon tax surcharge + exclusion from PSU procurement lists + MoEF&CC show-cause notice MEDIUM
